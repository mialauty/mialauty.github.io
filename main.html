Hello, beautiful.

Happy anniversary!

I've spent the last eight years in love with a girl, who falls asleep like a narcoleptic, and who hums random tunes subconsciously, and who cries when she laughs and laughs when she cries and laughs all the time (so she's always in a superposition of all states). This girl loves me too, and also salmon sushi, and ballet, and Grey's Anatomy, and Gong Yoo.

This girl is my light and my hope and my whole world. She is also the engine oil keeping my pistons torquing, the air-conditioning keeping me comfortable when I'm running hot, the crossword puzzle keeping my brain from alzheimers, and the sous-vide machine making sure I come out cooked all the way through.

The past eight years with you have been nothing short of magical. Life before you was like a Starbucks Verona: dark and full of terrors. Life is now like a Hacienda La Esmeralda Gesha: bright, sweet, juicy, and complex. I can't help but crave eighty more!

July 2011

Eight years ago, we started with a kiss, or two.

One, with Neon and Stop This Train and Gravity playing in the background, laying on a shallow mattress on the floor of a soon to be torn down apartment.

And another, of the chocolate variety, cheesily hidden and then buried and then (painfully) dug up, while eating dessert, after a purposefully event-less date.

Both times:

surprising, yet also earnestly craved.

Just like before, I have a surprise for you: I've been sneaking around the past few months...

... on clandestine missions to scout would-be locations...

... locations, where I have secretly been luring you to.

...

all, that I may show how much I love you.

and because eight years is long enough.
and I desperately crave eighty more.
